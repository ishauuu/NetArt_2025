An Exhibition: Modern Day Indian Culture Through The Lens of Social Media

Curated by Isha Umamageswaran

The reason why I chose these pieces is because they say a lot about the experience of the Gen-Z Indian diaspora. There is often a lot to unpack due to the dual identities that come with being children of immigrants. These pieces, specifically Brahma’s Homepage, Paper Samosa, and Envisioning the Hindu Divine, have all prompted a dialogue around the niche and somewhat-funny shared experiences and feelings of the young Indian-Americans. Brahma’s Homepage is more directed towards confusing identities about religion, while Paper Samosa and Envisioning the Human Divine bring humor into everyday occurrences. I really like this collection of works because the authors of the pieces employ different artistic strategies.

Brahma's Homepage

This website depicts what the author imagines the Hindu god Brahma’s “website” to look like, if he had one. It showcases a lot of religious imagery and tells the story of the author’s religious beliefs and journey.

Paper Samosa

This series of Net Art postings, on Instagram, shows the experience of Gen-Z Indian American through a comedic lens. I think it calls out a lot of necessary issues that exist in the diaspora and also creates a lot of important discourse among people who view it.
Envisioning the Hindu Divine
This website explores the representation of Hindu deities in modern media. It critiques the commercialization of religious imagery and its impact on cultural identity.
